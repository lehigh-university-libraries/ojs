Reviewer Guidelines

Thank you for serving as a reviewer for the Lehigh Rising Scholars Journal (LRSJ). These guidelines will help you structure your review, maintain professionalism, and support our mission of constructive, educational peer review.

Establish Your Expertise

  • Convey your qualifications and familiarity with the field
  • Summarize the manuscript’s topic and its relevance
  • Position the work within current literature and note if it advances the field

Craft the Summary

  • Highlight the main findings in a few sentences
  • Emphasize its importance to the research community
  • Provide context by referencing prior studies

Present Strengths and Weaknesses

Major Points

  • Essential issues that must be addressed before publication
  • Examples: gaps in logic, flawed design

Minor Points

  • Suggestions that improve clarity but aren’t deal-breakers
  • Examples: extra citation, phrasing suggestions

Organize Your Comments

  • Number comments sequentially
  • Keep one issue per number
  • Reference manuscript locations (page, paragraph, line)

Be Specific

  • “On page 4, paragraph 2, the authors should cite Smith et al. (2023)….”
  • “Figure 2’s caption lacks units; please specify ‘µM’.”

Demonstrate Professionalism

  • Focus on the work, not the authors
  • Avoid aggressive tone, caps, or excessive punctuation
  • Use constructive framing (“Consider clarifying…”)

Proofread Your Review

  • Check that all comments are clear and specific
  • Ensure grammar, spelling, and formatting are polished

Make a Publication Recommendation

  • Accept as is – no changes needed
  • Accept with minor revisions – localized fixes
  • Revise and resubmit – fundamental revisions required
  • Reject – major flaws make the work unsuitable
